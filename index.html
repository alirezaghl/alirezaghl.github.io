Alireza Ghalambor

I am a research assistant at the CMC Lab working on computational neuroscience and brain-inspired AI. My research focuses on understanding how neural networks learn and represent cognitive tasks, with applications to reinforcement learning and world models.

I received my Doctorate of Medicine from Ahvaz University of Medical Sciences, where I graduated in the top 20% of my class. Currently, I am working under the supervision of Shervin Safavi, investigating how different factors affect the dynamics of recurrent neural networks trained on cognitive tasks.

Google Scholar / Github / Email / ghalamboral [at] gmail (dot) com

Research

My research aims to understand how artificial neural networks can develop brain-like representations for complex cognitive tasks. I work on the following themes:

  • Understanding the dynamics of recurrent neural networks in cognitive tasks using dynamical similarity analysis
  • Developing brain-inspired architectures for reinforcement learning and world models
  • Investigating cognitive maps and spatial-temporal representations in neural networks
  • Bridging computational neuroscience and artificial intelligence
